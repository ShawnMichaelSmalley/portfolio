Huntr Challenge Coin

This was a project to take the concept of a "challenge coin" - popular in military culture as tokens of achievements - and have some manufactured to hand out at an information security conference. Many of the Huntr InfoSec team are or were at one time members of the armed forces and would be evangelists for our threat detection platforms at the trade shows.

The design began digitally with a graphic meant to incorporate the three tenants of "guided hunting" (monitoring your network and isolating threats):

PERCEPTION - Huntr allows users to gain insight into their network through a wide array of simple and intuitive tools. Perception can come from many sources; file introspection, triaging raw network logs, or combing through endpoint activity. Huntr provides a platform in which analysts can move freely from one data source to the next, and constantly improve their perceptions of all areas of their network.

KNOWLEDGE - Huntr equips an analyst with the tools necessary to continuously build understanding of adversaries or malicious intent from their perceptions. The culmination of knowledge in an environment is paramount to efficient and effective hunting operations.

ACTION - Once knowledge has been accrued, action can take place. Huntr provides mechanisms for live detections of network data, file triggering via signatures, or offloading a payload to the users endpoint of choice.

The pyramid's three sides represent the three tenants while the eye with the crosshair represents guided hunting as a whole. Once a digital design was approved it was taken to a manufacturer to press and enamel the finished product.

You can learn more about Huntr at www.Huntr.com

Custom Branded Google Cardboard VR Headset

We wanted something to give out at events or leave with clients that would be useful and fun enough that they'd be more likely to keep it around and show off to others. With VR just beginning to get popular we setup a portion of our booths to showcase some nicer Samsung VR headsets to show eventgoers what VR was all about. When they were done we'd encourage them to download a free app on the Apple or Android app store and take one of our free Google Cardboard compatible headsets. This quickly became a handout that people would ask about at later events so proved to be memorable and make a lasting impression.

The design itself was taken from the ad campaigns we had been running in trade media at the time to match our other branding. We had a company DodoCase send us design templates for their "P2" model and got to work. When we ordered about 100 headsets the cost ended at about $15 each, but since most of our trade shows were for brand-to-brand marketing it worked out that our select audience was worth it (including keeping some for ourselves and making sure our favorite clients got a few, too).

Stillwater Poster

An interior design client for Interior Gilt saw a t-shirt that had a map of a city (Dublin, Ireland I believe) that had all streets, rivers, and geography etched into a gold foil square. Being located in Stillwater, OK they wanted something similar but in poster form to hang above a table in their entryway. Their style was colorful and eclectic, so wanted something more like watercolor or paint splatters.

After visiting several geological and municipal websites for Stillwater I downloaded numerous PDF maps and worked to layer them on top of each other to act as cutouts. Streets were given a heavier stroke than elevation changes, and bodies of water were filled in. Once the layers were created a paint background was placed behind them. Due to the large numbers of shapes involved the file came out huge even when flattened and optimized. However, it printed wonderfully and the interior design project was a success.

Celebrity Pop Art Posters

S. Powell & Co., an upscale clothing boutique in Uptown OKC, wanted something loud and bold to spice up their typical mannequin displays in the front windows of the shop. There were six, four female and two male, and their idea was to hang celebrity photos so that they would take the place of where each mannequin's face would be. As the entire store's motif was based around a large, pink neon sign, and their entire storefront was vivid, the more striking each poster the better.

Finding useful photos of icons that are poster sized is tricky, but taking good (but small) pics and tracing them in Illustrator allowed them to be scaled up as vectors rather than worrying about pixellation and distortion when blown up to 18x24. Paint brush outlines, Warhol-style backgrounds, and cell shading color overlays brought it all together.

We went through several different color changes for each part though, starting tame and pastel at first and then getting more intense and bold as discussions evolved. their marketing coordinator kept saying "more, louder, make those colors POP" until the final product was sure to catch a pedestrian's attention and get them to check out the displays.

The store also ran flyers to different parts of town and social media campaigns each emphasizing a different celebrity as a discount code. If someone mentioned Whitney for example then they knew they heard about the event via social media whereas others might have been a flyer from a restaurant, gym, bar or dance studio. Having different calls to action allowed for easy tracking to determine how popular each opportunity was for them.
