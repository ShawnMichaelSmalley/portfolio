PL Supplements / NautixLabs

PL Supplements was a local pharmaceutical company who wanted to setup a whitelabel service where health shops and dispensaries could create their own branded versions of popular supplements by uploading their own custom labels and then order stock for their own shops. This way a health food store, for example, could carry their own product line of common items and still benefit from a large supplier's quality control and FDA-certified lot tracking and validation process.

This project started as a customization of their BigCommerce-hosted eCommerce website, but eventually we became aware that BigCommerce wasn't going to give them the workflow that they needed as far as letting clients upload custom logos, see a 3D rendering of the new bottle with their branding, and track the FDA-required quality control measures and certificates. So, an in-house, custom eCommerce solution was built instead. Though screenshots of this aren't available, our custom suite for them also included a custom iPad application that their warehouse staff would use to manage inventory as it went through various stages to stock the storefront, go through inspection and certification, and shipping and delivery.

OK Gazette Events App

The OK Gazette's website features a widget where visitors can search, favorite, and track upcoming events and venues. They sought out the app design services of my office, and we set out to make a prototype for a native mobile app that would pleasingly offer users the same functionality while also eventually giving the OK Gazette another option for future ad revenue generation.

A user would begin by logging in with an existing social media account. Huntr Public Website

Huntr was a startup information security company that offered a multi-component set of solutions to help systems administrators analyze and protect their network as well as respond to threats and vulnerabilities. In addition to designing their logo, branding, and trade show swag I was also asked to help build their marketing website to help deliver their products to the public.

Their solutions were offered in multiple optional components that can either be purchased all at once as a full suite or individually to complement an existing infrastructure so that the admin could keep their favorite tools and just use the available APIs to get everything working together. This meant that a visitor would need to know which components are right for them by determining where they fit among the philosophies of what it means to do a "guided hunt" for security problems. So, in addition to typical web content explaining the different levels, it was also decided that an online survey would be used to help a visitor determine what solutions are right for them and approximately how much it would cost (in addition to giving the site some lead capture functionality).

As this was my first Angular project I worked mostly on the frontend assets and styles and then coordinated with a team who were building out the service layers that would be used to collect the responses to the survey to make product suggestions and tally cost estimations. I'd construct dummy pages and provide naked and labeled mockups to instruct the back end developers which classes to use or which elements to target with their Angular modules for certain functionality.

S. Powell & Co. Placeholder Site

S. Powell & Co. is an upscale clothing boutique located in the trendy Uptown portion of OKC. Their styles range from tasteful and chic to pop art sensibilities with brignt colors. While they were under construction of their physical storefront they wanted an attractive landing page to connect to their social media accounts so they could begin to earn followers and buzz leading up to the grand opening. This temporary site would eventually be replaced with a full Shopify suite to integrate with their registers and inventory.

The first suggested mockup would include landing pages for each of their featured brands as well as a blogging functionality powered by Jekyl where their staff would post fashion advice or product reviews. These mockups were done before brand colors had been established. It was also determined that they did not plan to blog or create any article based content.

The second prototype brings in their newly decided brand colors and also an emphasis on this being a single-page website and hub for their social media experience. All navigation is replaced with social media links and all page content aside from the feature carousel has been setup as social media plugins to show the latest posts. The footer's links have also been updated to include the brand's story since there is no longer an "about" page.

The final mockup has only a few differences from the last, as all the main features had been agreed upon and settled into the previous draft. The only major change was the inclusion of a slider near the bottom which would include logos for each of their major brands. Interior Gilt webpage mockups

Interior Gilt is a luxury furniture store that specializes in high-end product lines, custom-made items, antiques, and interior design services. They use a software suite to manage their registers and warehouse inventory but it did not come with an eCommerce solution. Our developers had already built a custom in- house eCommerce platform when we worked with PL Supplements which was based upon .NET Framework, so we decided to use some of that same backbone to create a set of plugins for their existing software to allow online shopping.

The first proposed design was dark and leveraged some of the black and gold themed print materials with the subtle damask texture that they were running at the time.

They decided they wanted something different for their web presence and didn't necessarily want to coordinate with their print media. The entire reason black and dark schemes were used in print was for the contrast, but that didn't translate as well on the web where sites vary wildly. Instead, a cleaner overall look was desired and for the next mockup I decided to begin fleshing out the rest of the site's components.

The intention with this design would be that as you scroll down the navbar would dock at the top of the window and remain fixed. The featured header would keep the high contrast from the first draft, but would emphasize their Uptown OKC vibe with classic mural-style fonts over a brick backdrop. Scrolling down you can begin to see how products would appear as well as parallax calls to action, blog articles, and finally the footer.

Some of these design cues would continue through future drafts, but for the next few mockups you'll see where suggestions were implemented in a few different places in order to discuss other options and how certain themes could be carried into interior pages.

Lincoln Terrace Website Remodel

Lincoln Terrace is a historic neighborhood located adjascent to the state capitol complex in Oklahoma City. Their previous website was built using one of WordPress's default themes (I believe 2011 or another such free layout) but they had setup numerous custom apps within it to handle their roster of HOA members, photo galleries, blog posts, and document library. They also had several pages that were hosted on another sub-site which were just static HTML pages containing an archive of old historic photos broken down by each street and house along each street.

They had three main goals going into the project. First, they wanted to stick with WordPress and all of their custom apps but wanted a "fresh start" because they had too many admin users and couldn't recall all of the permissions they had handed out over the years. So, I'd migrate them to a second WordPress account which would also serve as our sandbox during the development phase so that their current site wouldn't be replaced until they were happy with their new one.

Their next goal was to modernize their website so that it worked great on mobile devices and included a simpler and easier to read interface. I tackled this by doing mockups on the designs until they liked how it was put together and then I created a custom WordPress theme setup just for them.

Their final goal was to migrate everything - including those old static HTML pages that were hosted elsewhere - under one roof so it could be managed in one place and kept up to date. Since many of those old pages were built using rigid HTML tables I setup Cherry Editor for them and made sure all pages were recreated using this same editor to lay out responsive grids with media break points that were easily seen in the editor. They could click on a column to edit that column's contents without fear of messing up those break points.

After using several WordPress migration plugins to make sure all image galleries, HOA member contacts, document uploads, blog posts (and any images and links within them), and static pages had all been moved over correctly and nothing errored as broken links they reviewed the new site and when approved we pointed their domain name at it and let the old WordPress account get deleted. It made for a smooth transition.
